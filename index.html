Methodology & Sources

Valuation Framework — Three-Method Blend

This tool blends three standard FIG valuation methodologies, weighted as follows:

Method Weight Formula
Price / Tangible Book Value (P/TBV) 40% TBV × dynamic P/TBV multiple
Price / Earnings (P/E) 30% LTM Net Income × dynamic P/E multiple
Core Deposit Premium 30% TBV + (Core Deposits × 7%)

The blended result is then multiplied by all applicable adjustment factors. A ±8% range is applied to the final weighted average to produce the high and low estimates.

Dynamic P/TBV Multiple

Rather than using a static P/TBV multiple, this tool derives it from the institution's Return on Equity (ROE), which is the primary driver of P/TBV multiples in bank M&A.

P/TBV = 1.0 + (ROE × 5.5)
Clamped: min 0.85x, max 2.20x

Example: ROE of 12% → P/TBV = 1.0 + (0.12 × 5.5) = 1.66x

Dynamic P/E Multiple

P/E = 19.0 + ((58 − Efficiency Ratio) / 5) × 0.5
Clamped: min 10.0x, max 26.0x

Adjustment Factors

Factor Basis Range
NPL Discount ≥2%: −10%. 1–2%: −5%. <1%: none. 0.90 – 1.00
Asset Size Premium Log-scale vs. $250M baseline. 0.95 – 1.10
ROA Premium Baseline 1.00%. ±1% per 0.10% variance. 0.90 – 1.10
NIM Adjustment Baseline 3.20%. ±3% per 1% NIM variance. 0.94 – 1.06
Loan/Deposit Ratio Sweet spot 70–90%: neutral (1.00). <60%: −4% (idle earning assets). 60–70%: −2% (under-deployed). 90–100%: −3% (funding pressure). >100%: −8% (wholesale funding reliance). 0.92 – 1.00
Efficiency Adjustment Standalone multiplier; every 4% from 58% baseline adds/subtracts ~1%. 0.92 – 1.08
